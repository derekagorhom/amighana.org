About Us

The african Maths Initiative will be a unique opportunity for Students in Ghana to have their eyes opened to the world of mathematics and advance technology.

AMI Ghana wants to develop a positive mathematical culture within the African continent. Through the Allotey Maths Camp, a one-week residential programme for SHS students, we showcase the practical and fun side of mathematics using ideas from programming to code breaking to modelling nature. Students eyes are opened up to the power, importance and fun of mathematics, completely changing their attitude from a fear to a love and enjoyment of mathematics. At the Allotely Maths Camp, everybody learns, with both international and local volunteers coming together every year to brainstorm new activities, puzzles and games to excite and engage students. After successfully running maths camps for six years, our Maths Clubs initiative is taking these ideas directly into schools, scaling up our activities, aiming to eventually reach all students in Ghana.

Mission

Partnering with relevant institutions to bridge the gap between research and practicality through Education Initiatives and technology.

Vision

To Create an environment where relevant education initiaitives and research yields results in terms of development.

AMI Ghana's overriding objective is to support the improvement of education and other scientific developmental projects, through the development of a positive mathematical culture and research initiatives at all academic levels within Ghana and the rest of the African continent. AMI Ghana will be involved in a variety of projects and initiaves related to one or more of the objectives:

1. Bring the latest mathematical knowledge and teaching methods to africa

2. Popularise mathematics to dispel the myth that maths is hard.

3. increase general access to scientific knowledge and education through mathematical initives.

4. collaborate with educational institutions and organisations to advise on mathematics curriculum development and ICT integration at all academic levels.
